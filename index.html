I wanted a bidet. I'm renting — I couldn't touch the plumbing, so that was that.

Then it clicked: the problem was never me, or you. It's the bathroom. Ours were designed for an era when water meant a plumber and a permanent fixture. Most of the world cleans with water — the West just built rooms that made it hard.

FAQ
Good questions.

How does it work without plumbing?

Fill it from the top, switch it on, and the electric pump pressurises the water. Lift the wand to rinse, then set it back. No pipes, no fixed connection.

Is it hygienic?

Food-grade materials throughout, and fresh water every time you fill it.

How do you clean it?

The lid lifts off so you can rinse the tank out whenever you like.

Is the water heated?

Not in this version — it runs at room temperature.

How is it powered?

A USB-C rechargeable battery, with weeks of use between charges.
